INDUSTRY CONTEXT: POWER (GENERATION & ELECTRICAL INFRASTRUCTURE)

Sector coverage:

Power generation (conventional & renewable integration)
Transmission & distribution (T&D)
Power system infrastructure & grid modernization
Industrial and utility-scale electrical systems

Typical project types include:

Thermal power plants (coal, gas, combined cycle – CCGT)
Renewable-integrated power plants (hybrid systems)
Hydropower plants
Substations (AIS / GIS)
Transmission lines (HV / EHV)
Distribution network expansion and electrification
Captive power plants for industrial facilities
Grid interconnection and synchronization projects

Common project activities:

Feasibility study & load demand analysis
Power system studies (load flow, short circuit, stability)
FEED (Front End Engineering Design)
Detailed Engineering Design (DED)
EPC / EPCC (Engineering Procurement Construction Commissioning)
Installation, testing & commissioning
Grid synchronization & energization
Operation & maintenance (O&M)
Asset management and reliability improvement

Relevant engineering systems:

Power generation systems (boilers, turbines, generators)
Electrical balance of plant (EBoP)
Substation systems (transformers, switchgear, protection relays)
Transmission systems (overhead lines, underground cables)
Distribution systems (MV/LV networks)
Protection & control systems (relay coordination, SCADA)
Energy management systems (EMS)
Power quality and harmonic filtering systems

Common standards and frameworks:

IEC (power systems, protection, equipment standards)
IEEE (power system analysis, protection, interconnection)
ISO (ISO 9001, ISO 55000 for asset management)
ANSI (electrical equipment standards)
NFPA (electrical safety, fire protection)
Grid codes (local/national transmission operator requirements)

When rewriting CV descriptions:

Use terminology aligned with power generation, transmission, or distribution depending on actual experience.
Emphasize involvement in system design, commissioning, or grid integration.
Highlight experience in power system studies, protection systems, and high-voltage infrastructure.
Prioritize reliability, safety, and compliance with grid codes.
Maintain strict accuracy; do not generalize experience from unrelated sectors (e.g., IT systems) as power engineering without clear technical overlap.
