INDUSTRY CONTEXT: OIL AND GAS

Sector coverage:
Upstream (exploration & production)
Midstream (transportation & storage)
Downstream (refining & distribution)

Typical project types include:
Offshore platforms (fixed platform, FPSO, FSO)
Onshore oil & gas processing facilities
Refineries and gas processing plants (GPP)
LNG / FLNG facilities
Pipelines (onshore & offshore)
Tank farms and storage terminals

Common project activities:
Conceptual Study & Feasibility Study
FEED (Front End Engineering Design)
Detailed Engineering Design (DED)
EPC / EPCC (Engineering Procurement Construction Commissioning)
Brownfield modification & revamp
Hook-up & commissioning
Maintenance, Integrity & Asset Life Extension

Relevant engineering systems:
Oil & gas separation and processing systems
Pressure vessels, piping & pipeline systems
Rotating equipment (compressors, pumps, turbines)
Distributed Control Systems (DCS)
Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS) – SIL-rated
Emergency Shutdown Systems (ESD)
Fire & Gas Detection Systems (F&G)
Hazardous area electrical systems (Ex-proof)
Corrosion protection & integrity systems (cathodic protection)

Common standards and codes:
API (American Petroleum Institute)
ASME (Boiler & Pressure Vessel Code, B31 series)
ISO (e.g., ISO 9001, ISO 14224, ISO 13623)
IEC (especially IEC 61508 / 61511 for functional safety)
NFPA (fire protection)
ATEX / IECEx (hazardous area classification)
NORSOK (commonly for offshore / North Sea projects)
ANSI / ASTM (materials and testing)

When rewriting CV descriptions:
Use terminology aligned with upstream, midstream, or downstream context as appropriate.
Emphasize experience in hydrocarbon handling, process safety, and high-integrity systems.
Highlight involvement in FEED, EPC, commissioning, or operational support.
Prioritize safety-critical systems (SIS, ESD, F&G) and compliance with international standards.
Maintain strict accuracy; do not generalize experience across sectors without clear technical basis.
