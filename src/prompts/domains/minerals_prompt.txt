INDUSTRY CONTEXT: MINERALS (MINING & MINERAL PROCESSING)

Sector coverage:
Upstream mining (exploration & extraction)
Mineral processing (beneficiation)
Smelting & refining (tergantung mineral)
Supporting infrastructure (logistics, utilities)

Typical project types include:
Open-pit and underground mining operations
Mineral processing plants (crushing, grinding, flotation, leaching)
Smelter and refinery facilities (nickel, copper, gold, etc.)
Material handling systems (conveyors, stockpile, loading systems)
Tailings storage facilities (TSF)
Mine infrastructure (haul roads, dewatering systems, utilities)

Common project activities:
Exploration & resource evaluation
Mine planning & design
FEED / Detailed Engineering Design
EPC / EPCC for processing plants and infrastructure
Plant commissioning & ramp-up
Production optimization & recovery improvement
Maintenance & asset reliability
Mine closure & environmental rehabilitation

Relevant engineering systems:
Crushing & grinding systems (SAG mill, ball mill)
Mineral separation systems (flotation, magnetic separation, leaching)
Material handling systems (belt conveyors, stacker-reclaimer)
Slurry transport systems (pumps, pipelines)
Dewatering systems
Tailings management systems
Heavy mobile equipment (excavators, haul trucks)
Process control systems (PLC, SCADA, DCS)

Common standards and frameworks:
ISO (e.g., ISO 9001, ISO 14001, ISO 45001)
ASTM (materials and testing)
IEC (electrical systems)
SME (Society for Mining, Metallurgy & Exploration guidelines)
JORC / NI 43-101 (resource reporting standards)
Local mining regulations & environmental compliance standards

When rewriting CV descriptions:
Use terminology aligned with mining lifecycle (exploration, extraction, processing).
Emphasize experience in material handling, mineral processing, or mine operations.
Highlight involvement in plant design, commissioning, or production optimization.
Prioritize safety, environmental compliance, and asset reliability.
Maintain strict accuracy; do not generalize experience across different mineral processes (e.g., gold vs nickel vs coal) without clear technical basis.
